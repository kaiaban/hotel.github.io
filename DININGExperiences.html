PANTRY

The pantry at the Daryl Hotel is a well-stocked and organized space where all the ingredients for the hotel's delicious meals are prepared. It is a busy place, with chefs and cooks constantly moving in and out, but it is also a place of creativity and passion.

The pantry is divided into different sections, each with its own purpose. There is a section for fresh produce, a section for meats and seafood, a section for dairy products, and a section for dry goods. There is also a large section for cooking equipment and utensils.

The pantry is staffed by a team of experienced and skilled chefs and cooks. They are responsible for preparing all of the hotel's meals, from breakfast to dinner. They also work with the hotel's banquet team to prepare food for special events.

PANTRY BAR

Nestled within the Daryl Hotel's vibrant ambiance, the Pantry Bar stands as a haven for both discerning palates and seekers of spirited camaraderie. Its rustic charm, infused with a touch of modern elegance, beckons guests to unwind and indulge in a symphony of flavors.

The bar's centerpiece, a magnificent mahogany counter, bears the marks of countless tales shared and laughter exchanged. Behind this stage of mixological artistry, skilled bartenders weave their magic, crafting cocktails that tantalize the senses and ignite conversations.

UMU

The Umu at the Daryl Hotel is a fine-dining restaurant that serves modern Filipino cuisine. The restaurant is located on the ground floor of the hotel and has a capacity of up to 50 guests.

The Umu's menu features a variety of dishes, including:

  • Kinilaw na Tuna: Fresh tuna marinated in vinegar, onions, and chili peppers.
  • Adobo na Baboy: Pork braised in soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic.
  • Sinigang na Baboy: Pork stew in sour broth with tomatoes, onions, and okra.
  • Kare-Kare: Oxtail stew in peanut sauce with vegetables.
  • Lechon Kawali: Deep-fried pork belly.

BENJARONG

The Benjarong menu features a wide variety of Thai dishes, from appetizers to desserts. Some of the most popular dishes include:

  • Tom Yum Goong: A spicy and sour soup with shrimp, lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves.
  • Pad Thai: Stir-fried rice noodles with shrimp, chicken, or tofu, eggs, peanuts, and bean sprouts.
  • Green Curry: A curry made with green chilies, coconut milk, and basil.
  • Massaman Curry: A curry made with coconut milk, potatoes, peanuts, and spices.
  • Mango Sticky Rice: A popular Thai dessert made with sweet sticky rice, ripe mangoes, and coconut milk.
